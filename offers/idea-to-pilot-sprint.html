idea-to-pilot sprint

one week to turn your AI idea into a working pilot — validated, functional, ready to demo.

the problem

you have an AI idea. maybe it's been in a slide deck for months. maybe it came out of a strategy session. maybe a founder sketched it on a napkin. either way — nobody's validated whether it actually works with real data, real users, real constraints.

you don't need a six-month roadmap. you need someone to build the thing in a week and tell you if it's viable.

what you get

a working pilot in one week:

  • functional AI system — not a mockup, not slides. a working system you can put in front of real users or stakeholders
  • scoped to the core value prop — we build the one thing that proves (or disproves) the idea, not the whole product
  • real data integration — connected to your actual data sources, not synthetic demos
  • viability assessment — honest evaluation of what works, what doesn't, and whether this should go to production
  • architecture brief — how it's built, what decisions were made, and what the path to production looks like if you decide to proceed

how it works

day 1 — scoping session (4 hours): we define the pilot together. what's the core hypothesis? what data exists? what's the simplest thing that proves the idea works? we start building live in the session.

days 2-4 — async build: i build the pilot. daily updates, you can steer as we go. i'll flag blockers early — if something about the idea doesn't hold up, you'll know on day 2, not day 10.

day 5 — demo + handoff (2 hours): we demo the pilot, review the viability assessment, and walk through the architecture. you leave with a working system and a clear picture of what comes next.
