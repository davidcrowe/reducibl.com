build vs buy analysis

a structured analysis of whether to build or buy AI capabilities — with cost models and a clear recommendation.

the problem

you're deciding whether to build AI capabilities in-house or buy from vendors. it's a high-stakes call — build when you should've bought and you burn engineering months. buy when you should've built and you're locked into a vendor that doesn't fit.

most teams make this decision based on vibes, vendor demos, or whoever talks loudest in the room. you need a framework — cost models, risk analysis, and a recommendation grounded in your actual constraints.

what you get

a build vs buy recommendation brief covering:

  • capability inventory — every AI capability mapped, with build and buy options for each
  • total cost of ownership — not just sticker price. engineering time, maintenance, integration costs, talent requirements for build; licensing, lock-in risk, migration costs for buy
  • risk analysis — what you control and what you don't under each scenario
  • vendor landscape — if buying, which vendors are credible for your use cases and which are selling demos
  • decision framework — a repeatable model your team can use for future build/buy decisions, not just this one
  • recommendation brief — clear recommendation per capability, with reasoning your leadership can act on

how it works

week 1 — discovery: i interview key stakeholders and your engineering team to understand the capabilities you need, your current stack, team capacity, and constraints. i also evaluate the vendor options in your space.

week 2 — analysis: i build the cost models, map the decision framework, evaluate vendors, and draft the recommendation brief. you get a draft to review before the workshop.

week 3 — workshop session (3 hours): we walk through the analysis with your leadership and engineering leads. pressure-test the assumptions, debate the hard calls, and align on a path forward.
